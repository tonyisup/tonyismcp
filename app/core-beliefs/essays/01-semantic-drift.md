# Semantic Drift Is the Rift

Every person has a slightly different definition of every word. Even two children who learn the same word at the same time will carry it into subtly different conceptual worlds — because their experiences never stop reshaping what those words mean.

This isn't just an observation about language. It's the root problem. I believe most conflicts — personal, political, professional — are not disagreements about facts. They are disagreements hiding behind shared vocabulary. Two people say the same word, feel like they're talking about the same thing, and are actually navigating entirely different semantic maps.

A shared label is not proof of a shared concept.

A word is a hook. What hangs on the hook depends on everything that person has ever lived through. The word "freedom" hooks entirely different objects in two different minds. The word "respect" hooks different expectations, different memories, different pain thresholds. Same hook, different weight.

This belief is what drives my work on App Spec Generator, Semantics, and the Semantic Abstraction Graph. Not because I want perfect definitions. Because I want better bridges. Between all of us, including AI.
