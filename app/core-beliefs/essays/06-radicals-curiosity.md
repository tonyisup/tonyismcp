# Radically Curious — And It Hurts

I am curious to a fault. I will spiral into understanding the polysemy distribution of English nouns before I ship the feature. It has cost me momentum, clean weekends, and finished products more times than I'd like to admit.

But I also believe this curiosity is the engine. Every serious thing I've built started with a spiral — a refusal to accept surface-level explanations. The problem isn't the spiral. The problem is not knowing when to land.

I try to hold both at once: dig deeper than anyone else in the room, then force myself to *use* what I found. Curiosity without creation is consumption. Creation without curiosity is craft without vision. The tension is the point — and it's a tension I navigate badly as often as I navigate it well.

The discipline I'm still learning: curiosity is a compass, not a map. It tells you where to look. It doesn't tell you when to stop.
