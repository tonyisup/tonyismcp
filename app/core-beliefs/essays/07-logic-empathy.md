# Logic-Driven, Empathy-Protected

I default to logic in decision-making, but I refuse to wield it as a cudgel. Logic is a scalpel — precise, useful, and capable of immense harm when pointed at someone's lived experience. I will not use analysis as an excuse to invalidate someone's pain.

Empathy has boundaries, too. I will not sacrifice my own mental health to absorb someone else's discomfort. But within those boundaries, I try to care genuinely — about people's positions, their friction points, and the emotional truth underneath their words.

This connects directly to the semantic drift work: most people are trying to say something real. The question is whether I'm listening to what they're *actually* saying, or just processing the surface syntax and responding to my own interpretation of it.

The hardest skill I'm still learning: disagreeing with someone's conclusion while fully respecting the experiential path that led them there. That's not tolerance. That's precision.
